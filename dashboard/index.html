FAUSTINI EXPLORATION - JUDGE'S GUIDE

CLASSIFICATION: EVALUATION GUIDE

1. Integrated Software Platforms & GIS Engines

Our pipeline is built on professional spaceborne radar processing tools. We use MIDAS for DFSAR polarimetric decomposition, ENVI for initial image restoration, QGIS/ArcGIS for South Polar stereographic georeferencing, and Python (NumPy, SciPy, Rasterio, GDAL) for spatial co-registration and A* traverse calculations.

2. Scientific Ice Detection Approach & Statistical Robustness

High CPR alone does not prove ice since blocky ejecta raises CPR. We filter out rocks and false positives using a strict multi-modal criteria: CPR > 1.0 AND DOP < 0.13, cross-correlated with surface roughness. The dashboard displays the correlation matrix confirming we have decoupled slopes from radar anomalies.

3. Volumetric Stratigraphy & L-Band Reconstruction

The stratigraphic panel shows subsurface layers along the path. The new L-Band Expected Ice Imagery panel shows a 2D reconstructed depth slice of ice concentration derived using dielectric constant models (\(\epsilon_r' \approx 4.5\) for ice-rich regolith vs. \(3.0\) for dry dust).

4. Safe landing spot (LZ-A) & Detailed Imagery

LZ-A is situated on the flat crater rim (slope < 3.5°). The Close-up monitor renders the raw high-resolution OHRC surface imagery of this landing zone with a safety landing ellipse reticle.

5. Traverse Optimization & Bayesian Classification

The footer planners (A*, D*, RRT*) route dynamically around hazards. The Bayesian source classifier computes probability updates for competing hypotheses (Subsurface Ice vs. Blocky Ejecta vs. Frost vs. Noise).
